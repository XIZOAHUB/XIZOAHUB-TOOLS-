How to Tune Your Guitar?

Tuning is the first skill every guitarist must learn. This tool uses Standard Tuning (E-A-D-G-B-E).

  • Step 1: Click the Low E button above. Listen carefully to the tone.
  • Step 2: Pluck your guitar's thickest string. Turn the tuning peg until your string's sound matches the tool's sound perfectly.
  • Step 3: Repeat for all strings (A, D, G, B, High E).
  • Tip: Always tune up to the note. If you are too high, go lower than the note and tighten it back up. This keeps the string stable.

Beginner's Guide to Chords

Chords are the building blocks of songs. The chart above shows Open Chords, which are played near the headstock.

Reading the Diagram: The vertical lines are strings (Left is Thick E, Right is Thin E). The horizontal spaces are frets. The Cyan Dots show where to place your fingers.

3 Chords to Learn First:

  • G Major: The most popular chord in pop/rock.
  • C Major: Used in almost every happy song.
  • D Major: Essential for acoustic ballads.
