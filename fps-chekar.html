Why FPS Matters for Gaming?

FPS (Frames Per Second) determines how smoothly a game or video plays on your screen. The higher the FPS, the smoother the visual experience. In competitive gaming (like Valorant, CS:GO, BGMI), FPS is crucial because it reduces input lag.

This tool by Xizoa Hub allows you to check your monitor's Real Refresh Rate (Hz) and browser performance instantly. We support detection up to 240Hz and 360Hz monitors.

Understanding Hz vs. FPS

Many people confuse these two terms. Let's break it down:

  • FPS (Frames Per Second): This is how many images your GPU (Graphics Card) produces every second.
  • Hz (Hertz): This is how many times your Monitor updates the screen every second.

Pro Tip: If your PC generates 200 FPS but your monitor is only 60Hz, you will only see 60 frames. You need a high-refresh-rate monitor (144Hz or 240Hz) to see the benefit of high FPS.

How to Increase FPS? (Optimization Guide)

1. Update Graphics Drivers

Always keep your NVIDIA or AMD drivers updated. New updates often contain optimizations for the latest games.

2. Disable V-Sync

Vertical Sync locks your FPS to your monitor's refresh rate. While it stops screen tearing, it increases input lag. Turn it OFF for competitive gaming.

3. Close Background Apps

Browsers like Chrome consume heavy RAM. Use our tool to check if closing tabs improves your system's responsiveness.

Input Lag & Latency

Low FPS results in high "Frame Time". If your game runs at 30 FPS, a new frame appears every 33.3ms. At 144 FPS, a new frame appears every 6.9ms. This difference is why pros react faster on high-end PCs.
