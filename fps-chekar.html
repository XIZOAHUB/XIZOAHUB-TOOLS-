Comprehensive Guide to FPS and Refresh Rate

In the world of competitive gaming and high-performance computing, numbers matter. FPS (Frames Per Second) and Hz (Hertz) are the two most critical metrics that define the smoothness of your visual experience. Xizoa Hub provides a professional-grade online tool to measure these metrics accurately without installing any software.

What is FPS and Why Does It Matter?

FPS represents how many unique images your graphics card (GPU) can render every second. Higher FPS results in smoother animations and lower input lag. For gamers, this is crucial:

  • 30 FPS: Minimum for playable console gaming.
  • 60 FPS: The standard for smooth gameplay.
  • 144+ FPS: Competitive standard for shooters like Valorant and CS:GO.

Understanding Screen Refresh Rate (Hz)

While FPS is generated by your computer, Hz is the limit of your monitor. A 60Hz monitor can only display 60 images per second, even if your game is running at 200 FPS. This mismatch can cause "Screen Tearing". Our tool helps you verify if your monitor is actually running at its advertised 120Hz, 144Hz, or higher.

How to Use the Ghosting Test?

Ghosting occurs when pixels cannot change colors fast enough to keep up with moving objects. Use the "Motion Blur Test" section above. Slide the speed to maximum and follow the rocket. if you see a blurry trail behind it, your monitor has a high response time (GtG).

Troubleshooting Low FPS

If you are seeing lower numbers than expected:

  • Ensure "Hardware Acceleration" is enabled in your browser settings.
  • Plug in your laptop charger (running on battery often limits FPS to 30).
  • Close other heavy tabs or applications.
